Verification and Theorem Proving for Continuous Systems

NETCA Workshop

August 26, 2005
Buttery room, Wolfson College
Oxford UK

Aims and Scope

The UK Network in Computer Algebra (NETCA) is organising a workshop on various aspects of continuous systems verification, mainly with the focus on theorem proving. The workshop will take place on the day after TPHOLs and CSL, and is intended as a forum for work in progress on new and emerging areas to be presented and discussed, and experiences to be shared.

The organisers welcome submissions of extended abstracts or draft papers on all aspects of verification of continuous systems and theorem proving for continuous mathematics, both theoretical work and applications. The topics related to the workshop include (but are not limited to) the following:
  • Computational models for continuous and hybrid systems
  • Verification of continuous systems (e.g. control systems and embedded systems)
  • Applications of theorem provers to continuous systems
  • Formalisation of continuous mathematical theories such as real analysis


Registration

Participation at the workshop is free of charge. We require, however, that you confirm participation by sending an email to the organisers at pbo@dcs.qmul.ac.uk, as the workshop can accomodate a limit of 50 participants. A buffet lunch will be provided to all registered participants.
Final date for registration is 22nd August (Monday) until 2pm.

Organization

The workshop is affiliated with TPHOLs 2005. The workshop chair is Graham White, and organisers Hanne Gottliebsen, Max Kanovich and Paulo Oliva of the Computer Science Department at Queen Mary, University of London.
